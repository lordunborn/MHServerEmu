Server Systems Guide

Custom features on this server and how to use them. Type !commands in game for the full command list.

Dinos Invade Manhattan new game mode

Talk to Shanna in Avengers Tower to head into a wave-survival fight against a dinosaur invasion — seven escalating waves of raptors, pterosaurs, and cliffwalkers, capped off with a boss fight against King Lizard (or his Rider, picked at random) in a purpose-built arena.

Watch the threat meter on screen — it climbs automatically the longer you're in there, and maxing it out ends the run early. Killing wave mobs chips it down a little; grabbing the power-up orb that spawns periodically is the real way to keep it under control.

Locked to Heroic difficulty so parties always land in the same instance together. Phantom Heroes and Rogue Nemesis won't show up in this region, so the fight stays balanced around your actual group size.

Rogue Encounters opt-in, then automatic

Once you opt in, villains from your hero's Rogues Gallery will periodically ambush you out in the world. Every time you defeat one, their obsession with you grows — their Nemesis Rank climbs, and they come back tougher and more rewarding next time. Lose to one and they only back off by a single rank, not all the way to zero, so a rank you're not quite ready for yet is a setback, not a full restart.

RankWhat to expect
0 – 1Easy — a warm-up fight, shouldn't feel threatening.
2 – 3A real step up — pay attention.
4Genuinely dangerous if you're undergeared.
5Can be lethal if you don't know your kit or aren't geared for it. Not raid-boss hard, but not a fight to walk into blind.
  • !rogue enable / !rogue disable — opt in or out of Rogue Encounter ambushes.
  • !rogue status — see your current opt-in state, any active encounter, and your Nemesis Rank against every villain you've fought.

Your character needs to be level 30+ to be targeted. A villain you've beaten to Rank 5 goes on cooldown against you for the rest of the day after that win, so it can't just be farmed back-to-back — it'll be available again the next day.

Incursion Events admin-triggered

From time to time, an admin may trigger an Incursion — a wave of hostile invaders (each one a familiar hero or villain's face, but not the real deal) that appear and hunt players down in patrol zones. This isn't a bug or a scripting glitch if you see it happening — it's a deliberate, temporary event. Defeating an invader drops loot just like any other tough enemy.

There's no opt-in for this one — if it's running, it's running server-wide. It's not always on; admins turn it on and off as an event.

Phantom Heroes

Spawn server-side AI-controlled hero companions that fight alongside you as a synthetic party. Great for playing solo without actually playing alone.

  • !phantom spawn [count|heroname] [level] — spawn phantoms (default 4, up to 9 active at once). They auto-level with you unless you set a level.
  • !phantom squad save/spawn/list/delete [name] — save and reload favorite phantom lineups.
  • !phantom costume [hero] [random|costumename] — change how a phantom looks.
  • !phantom gear [hero] — re-roll a phantom's gear.
  • !phantom clear — despawn all your phantoms.

Can't be used while you're in combat.

Server-Side Loot Filter

Tell the server not to bother dropping loot you don't want — cleaner drops, less inventory clutter. Covers Rings, Medals, Insignias, Team-Up Gear, Catalysts, and Uru-Forged items.

  • !filter set <type> <rarity|on/off> [global|me|charactername] — set a rarity floor (e.g. no Rings below Rare) or turn a category off, scoped globally, to your current character, or a named one.
  • !filter list — see your current filters.
  • !filter clear <type> [scope] / !filter clearall — remove filters.
  • !filter rarities — list valid rarity names.

Item Auto-Pickup

Automatically picks up currency, crafting ingredients, Runeword Glyphs, and Relics within a radius so you're not manually clicking every drop. Also works together with Stash Affinity below — auto-picked-up items get routed to the right stash tab automatically.

  • !autopickup list — see your effective settings per category.
  • !autopickup set <currency|crafting|glyph|relic> <on|off|stash|inventory> — opt out of a category, or choose stash vs. inventory as the destination.
  • !autopickup clear <category> — revert to the server default.

Stash Affinity automatic — no commands

When you right-click an item and choose "Send to Stash" (the quick-move action), it automatically redirects to the best-matching tab instead of your default stash: a character-specific stash for bound items, or a tab whose name matches the item's type, with a "Catch All" tab as the fallback. It also applies when Auto-Pickup routes an item into a stash. Manually dragging an item to a tab you chose yourself is always respected as-is and never overridden.

Nothing to configure — this is just why a quick-stashed or auto-picked-up item sometimes lands in a different tab than expected.
